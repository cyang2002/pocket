## Design Context

### Users
Rewards hobbyists — people who actively optimize credit card spend across categories. They know the domain (what 5x means, why dining vs. travel matters), they have multiple cards, and they're here to make fast, confident decisions about which card to reach for. Speed and clarity matter more than hand-holding. This is primarily a personal-use tool that may expand to other hobbyists.

### Brand Personality
**Precise · Efficient · Clean**

The interface should feel like a well-crafted reference tool — something you'd trust to have the right answer. Not flashy, not loud. Confident in its own restraint. The equivalent of a really good reference book: clear hierarchy, data you can scan in seconds, nothing wasted.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode, editorial. Strong typographic hierarchy where the data does the talking. Color is used as signal — to highlight the best earn rate in a category, flag staleness, indicate selection — never as decoration. Structure is tight and deliberate. Think: the Financial Times or Stratechery. Warm enough not to feel cold, structured enough to scan fast.

**Anti-references:**
- Generic SaaS dashboard (sidebar nav, purple/blue gradients, dark mode glowing borders, metric hero cards)
- Spreadsheet-lite (data dumped in a table with zero design consideration — no hierarchy, no signal)
- Playful/consumer app (rounded everything, bright colors, over-casual tone)
- NerdWallet/Credit Karma (marketing-forward, ad-heavy, ranking CTAs)

### Design Principles
1. **Data first, chrome last** — every visual element must serve the data; decorative chrome earns its place or gets cut
2. **Color as signal** — use color to mark the winner in a category, flag stale data, indicate active selection; never use it purely for aesthetics
3. **Editorial hierarchy** — type size, weight, and spacing must clearly communicate what to read first; earn rates are the hero, not headings about earn rates
4. **Rewards-literate voice** — write for people who know what a sign-up bonus is; don't over-explain the domain
5. **Scannable over beautiful** — a hobbyist cross-referencing 8 cards for a restaurant bill needs to find "dining: 4x" in under a second; optimize for that
